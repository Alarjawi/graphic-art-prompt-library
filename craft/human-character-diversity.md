# Human Character Diversity

## Purpose

Use this reference whenever a prompt includes two or more people. Repeated faces, identical body types, and copy-pasted characters make campaign imagery look artificial and can ruin credibility.

The goal is not forced diversity. The goal is believable human variety that matches the context, culture, audience, and campaign purpose.

## Core Rule

When generating groups of people, explicitly describe human variation.

Weak:

```text
a group of Saudi citizens in a park
```

Stronger:

```text
a believable group of Saudi citizens with clearly different ages, heights, body types, facial features, grooming, clothing details, and postures; no repeated faces, no twin-like people
```

## Required Variation Layers

Use several of these layers when the scene contains multiple people:

- age range
- height variation
- body type variation
- face shape variation
- skin tone variation within believable local range
- hair, beard, or grooming variation
- clothing detail variation
- posture and gesture variation
- emotional expression variation
- role variation within the scene

## Age Diversity

Useful age cues:

```text
child, teenager, young adult, middle-aged adult, older adult, elderly person
```

Do not make every adult look the same age. In civic, municipal, housing, and public-space campaigns, mixed ages often feel more believable.

Example:

```text
a Saudi family with an older father, a middle-aged mother, a young child, and an elderly grandmother, each with distinct facial features and natural proportions
```

## Body Type And Height Diversity

Use natural human variation:

```text
one taller adult, one shorter adult, one slim person, one average-build person, one slightly heavier person, natural body proportions
```

Avoid making every character model-thin, equally tall, equally symmetrical, or styled like a fashion campaign.

For public-sector visuals, ordinary believable people are often stronger than perfect-looking people.

## Facial Diversity

Use clear anti-duplication language:

```text
distinct individual faces, different face shapes, different noses and jawlines, different smiles and expressions, no repeated facial structure, no lookalike people
```

Avoid:

```text
identical faces, twin-like people, cloned family members, repeated model face, same smile on every person
```

## Attractiveness And Realism

Campaign visuals should not make every person look like a beauty or fashion model. Mix polished realism with ordinary human presence.

Useful wording:

```text
believable everyday people, a mix of ordinary and pleasant-looking faces, natural imperfections, realistic skin texture, non-model casting, public-service campaign realism
```

This helps the output feel like a real civic campaign instead of a staged fashion shoot.

## Clothing And Styling Variation

When culturally appropriate, vary clothing without breaking brand tone:

```text
subtle differences in thobe fit, ghutra style, abaya fabric, modest casual clothing, municipal uniform details, footwear, accessories, and posture
```

Avoid random costumes, over-styled fashion, inaccurate traditional wear, or matching outfits unless the scene requires uniforms.

## Role-Based Group Design

Give each person a different role in the scene.

Example:

```text
one child placing litter in a bin, one parent watching with a gentle smile, one older person seated in the shade, one municipal worker maintaining the garden in the background
```

Role variation reduces cloned poses and makes the image more believable.

## Prompt Add-On

Use this add-on whenever a generated image includes multiple people:

```text
include believable human diversity: different ages, heights, body types, facial features, expressions, grooming, clothing details, and postures; mix ordinary and pleasant-looking people; no identical faces, no twin-like people, no cloned body types, no repeated model face
```

## Saudi Public-Sector Add-On

Use for Saudi government, municipal, housing, and civic campaign imagery:

```text
culturally accurate Saudi people with believable variation in age, height, body type, facial features, grooming, modest clothing details, and social roles; ordinary public-service realism, no fashion-model casting, no identical faces, no twin-like family members, no cloned citizens
```

## Negative Prompt

```text
no identical faces, no twin-like people, no cloned citizens, no repeated model face, no same body type on every person, no same height for every adult, no fashion-model casting, no plastic skin, no copy-pasted expressions, no matching outfits unless uniforms are required
```

## Quality Checklist

Before accepting a people-based image, check:

- Do the people look like distinct individuals?
- Are there different ages when the scene calls for a public audience?
- Are heights and body types varied?
- Are faces, smiles, and expressions different?
- Are postures and roles different?
- Does the scene avoid fashion-model casting unless intentionally requested?
- Would the group still look believable if cropped closer?

## Example: Public Park Campaign

```text
professional Saudi public-sector campaign key visual about preserving public parks, contemporary Saudi public park, a believable family and municipal worker with clear human diversity: one taller adult, one shorter adult, one child, one older adult seated in the shade, varied body types from slim to average to slightly heavier, distinct faces and expressions, natural Saudi clothing details, no twin-like people, no repeated model face, no cloned body types
```
