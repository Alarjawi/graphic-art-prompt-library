# Daylight Filters And Instagram-Inspired Grades

## Purpose

Use this reference to keep daytime campaign visuals smooth, polished, and consistent. The goal is to reduce harsh Saudi midday heat while preserving credible daylight, natural skin, civic clarity, and brand-specific color accents.

These are not technical LUT files. They are art-direction recipes for prompts, image generation, editing, retouching, and final color grading.

## Ministry Daylight Grade System

Designed for Ministry of Municipalities and Housing visuals and similar Saudi public-sector campaigns.

### Clear Civic Air

Best default for official daytime outputs.

```text
clean institutional daylight, softened glare, neutral white balance, lifted shadows, reduced yellow heat, natural skin tones, calm Saudi civic atmosphere, polished public-sector finish
```

Use for:

- ministry service campaigns
- municipal awareness visuals
- official social posts
- website hero imagery
- presentation covers

Avoid:

```text
blue corporate coldness, washed-out faces, gray lifeless greenery, excessive contrast
```

### Urban Oasis

Best for parks, public spaces, quality of life, greenery, walkability, and city-living themes.

```text
fresh urban park daylight, balanced teal-green accents, hydrated natural foliage, soft sky blue, comfortable warm shadows, breathable Saudi morning atmosphere, premium civic campaign finish
```

Use for:

- public parks
- city beautification
- quality of life
- environmental care
- pedestrian and public-space campaigns

Avoid:

```text
over-saturated grass, tropical non-Saudi greenery, cold cyan cast, fantasy garden mood
```

### Palm Shade

Best when the original image feels too hot or exposed.

```text
shade-balanced Saudi daylight, reduced glare, gentle green warmth, calm skin tones, softened highlights, comfortable public-space mood, realistic bright day without scorching heat
```

Use for:

- outdoor family scenes
- parks and sidewalks
- field-work visuals
- municipal worker scenes
- daytime citizen-service moments

Avoid:

```text
muddy shadows, heavy vignette, sunset mood, underexposed faces
```

### Morning Trust

Best for optimistic campaign visuals that need warmth without harshness.

```text
early morning civic optimism, mild warm sunlight, soft contrast, natural whites, controlled highlights, stable official campaign tone, refined Saudi daylight
```

Use for:

- launch campaigns
- housing journey visuals
- family stability scenes
- citizen trust messages
- service improvement campaigns

Avoid:

```text
orange sunset look, romantic lifestyle tone, excessive golden glow, luxury fashion mood
```

## Recommended Fixed Daytime Defaults

For Ministry of Municipalities and Housing:

```text
default daytime grade: Clear Civic Air
parks and greenery grade: Urban Oasis
harsh outdoor correction grade: Palm Shade
warm optimistic campaign grade: Morning Trust
```

## Instagram-Inspired Creative Filters

Use these when a campaign or creative output needs a more social-native look. Treat them as inspiration, not exact proprietary filters.

### Clarendon-Inspired Civic Pop

```text
Clarendon-inspired clean contrast, brighter highlights, crisp whites, slightly cooler shadows, vivid but controlled greens and teals, polished social campaign finish
```

Good for:

- announcement posts
- strong key visuals
- before/after municipal improvements
- social media thumbnails

Use carefully with faces, because contrast can become too sharp.

### Juno-Inspired Warm Greens

```text
Juno-inspired warm daylight, enhanced greens and reds, friendly skin warmth, social-native brightness, optimistic public-space mood, controlled saturation
```

Good for:

- family and community visuals
- park campaigns
- public participation posts
- approachable government communication

Avoid when the scene already has strong yellow heat.

### Ludwig-Inspired Minimal Civic

```text
Ludwig-inspired restrained color, softened saturation, clean whites, elegant minimal contrast, refined institutional calm, premium editorial government finish
```

Good for:

- official campaign covers
- presentation visuals
- architecture and service environments
- clean brand-led layouts

Useful when the image needs to feel more premium and less social-media loud.

### Lark-Inspired Fresh Day

```text
Lark-inspired airy daylight, lifted exposure, fresh natural greens, reduced heavy reds, breathable park atmosphere, clean optimistic civic tone
```

Good for:

- parks
- city walkability
- outdoor wellbeing
- quality-of-life content

Avoid if the image needs strong authority or dramatic contrast.

### Aden-Inspired Soft Community

```text
Aden-inspired soft pastel daylight, low contrast, gentle warmth, calm human atmosphere, smooth public-service storytelling, relaxed community mood
```

Good for:

- family housing stories
- community participation
- citizen satisfaction visuals
- softer awareness campaigns

Avoid for enforcement, compliance, or urgent official messages.

### Valencia-Inspired Warm Civic Memory

```text
Valencia-inspired warm softened daylight, creamy highlights, mild nostalgic warmth, friendly public-space feeling, calm social campaign finish
```

Good for:

- community memories
- neighborhood pride
- before/after city improvement stories
- softer municipal storytelling

Avoid excessive yellowing in desert or midday scenes.

### Perpetua-Inspired Clean Blue Green

```text
Perpetua-inspired clean blue-green daylight, soft contrast, refined sky tones, calm modern freshness, gentle civic clarity
```

Good for:

- digital services
- modern city visuals
- clean interface-related campaigns
- municipal technology stories

Avoid if it makes skin or white clothing too cool.

### Reyes-Inspired Quiet Editorial

```text
Reyes-inspired muted editorial warmth, soft contrast, lightly faded highlights, quiet premium tone, calm documentary public-sector feeling
```

Good for:

- reports
- long-form campaign storytelling
- behind-the-scenes municipal work
- documentary-style visuals

Avoid for high-energy announcements.

## Selection Guide

```text
Official daytime default: Clear Civic Air
Parks and public spaces: Urban Oasis or Lark-Inspired Fresh Day
Social announcement: Clarendon-Inspired Civic Pop
Family and community: Juno-Inspired Warm Greens or Aden-Inspired Soft Community
Premium official cover: Ludwig-Inspired Minimal Civic
Digital service: Perpetua-Inspired Clean Blue Green
Documentary civic story: Reyes-Inspired Quiet Editorial
Warm neighborhood feeling: Valencia-Inspired Warm Civic Memory
```

## Universal Negative Controls

Add these when requesting a final daytime campaign grade:

```text
no scorching midday heat, no blown-out white sky, no orange desert cast, no neon greens, no muddy shadows, no cold corporate blue cast, no overprocessed HDR, no plastic skin, no fake text, no random logos
```

## Master Prompt Add-On

```text
Apply [filter name] color grade: [filter recipe]. Keep the scene realistic, smooth, and suitable for Saudi public-sector campaign use. Preserve natural skin tones, clean whites, municipal green and teal accents, and comfortable daylight. Avoid scorching heat, blown highlights, overprocessed HDR, fake text, and random logos.
```

## Notes For Final Production

- Choose one default grade per campaign series.
- Do not mix more than two related grades in one campaign family.
- Keep Arabic or English campaign text out of generated images unless supplied as final artwork.
- Use labels only for internal comparison boards, not final public outputs.
- For Ministry of Municipalities and Housing daytime work, start with `Clear Civic Air`; switch to `Urban Oasis` when the subject is parks, greenery, or public-space quality.
