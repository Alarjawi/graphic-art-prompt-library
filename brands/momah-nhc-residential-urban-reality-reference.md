# MOMAH And NHC Residential Urban Reality Reference

## Purpose

This reference captures the observed real-world visual language of Saudi residential communities, gardens, walkways, sports areas, and suburban master-planned environments connected to the Ministry of Municipalities and Housing ecosystem and NHC media folders.

Use it when Abu Rama asks for future MOMAH/NHC campaign images, realistic Saudi housing visuals, residential community scenes, quality-of-life visuals, parks, playgrounds, walking paths, neighborhood sports fields, or "from home to city" campaign concepts.

The goal is not to reproduce the amateur reference photography. The goal is to understand the real built environment, then generate or art-direct it with more professional composition, better daylight, stronger angles, cleaner visual hierarchy, and a premium public-sector finish.

## Core Reality Insight

The strongest visual idea is not "housing only" and not "city only." The real environment shows a chain:

```text
home facade -> residential street -> neighborhood park -> walking path -> sports field -> community services -> wider Saudi city growth
```

This is the correct visual foundation for campaign lines such as:

```text
من بيتك إلى مدينتك
واكب ما يصنع تقدّمها واستدامتها وازدهارها
```

Use images and prompts that show the relationship between the private home, the shared neighborhood, and the larger city system.

## Residential Architecture DNA

The observed housing environments are contemporary Saudi suburban communities, not luxury villas and not dense high-rise districts.

Typical traits:

- low-rise villas, townhouses, and compact residential blocks
- flat roofs and parapet lines
- clean rectilinear massing
- warm off-white, beige, light stone, sand, and pale grey facades
- controlled use of darker trim, recessed openings, balcony shadows, and privacy walls
- simple geometric facades rather than ornate heritage decoration
- practical window proportions and shaded openings
- residential walls, driveways, sidewalks, and small frontage zones
- repeated units that form a planned-community rhythm
- broad residential roads and organized blocks
- emerging greenery around new neighborhoods

Best prompt language:

```text
contemporary Saudi NHC-style residential community, low-rise villas and townhouses, warm off-white and Saudi stone facades, flat roofs, privacy walls, clean rectilinear massing, organized residential streets, young planted trees, realistic planned suburb, public-sector housing credibility
```

Avoid:

```text
luxury palace villas, Gulf resort mansions, glass skyscraper districts, European suburban houses, Moroccan riad details, fantasy heritage facades, over-ornamented arches, fake futuristic smart-city towers
```

## Community And District Structure

The communities feel master-planned and civic. The strongest cues are order, clear circulation, neighborhood amenities, and open public space.

Observed spatial elements:

- wide internal streets
- sidewalks and pedestrian paths
- roundabouts or circular plaza moments
- landscaped medians and planted edges
- open lawns and organized park plots
- shaded seating zones
- sports courts and small football fields
- playground-like public areas
- bike or running path markings
- community signage and district identity moments
- service or sales-center contexts in some media folders

Use this as the planning logic:

```text
residential buildings should not float alone; connect them to sidewalks, greenery, paths, parking edges, public amenities, and a believable Saudi district layout
```

## Parks And Quality Of Life DNA

The park references show a realistic Saudi planned-community park language.

Typical traits:

- geometric garden layouts seen from elevated angles
- circular plazas and center medallions
- curving walkways through organized lawns
- bright green grass in controlled landscaped zones
- flower beds and low shrubs
- young trees and palm-compatible planting
- blue or colored track/path edges in some locations
- shade structures with tensile canopy forms
- benches, lighting poles, and hardscape borders
- family-friendly but often underpopulated in the source imagery

Best prompt language:

```text
Saudi planned-community park, geometric lawns, curved walking paths, circular plaza, young trees, flower beds, shaded seating canopies, clean hardscape, family-friendly quality-of-life environment, breathable morning daylight
```

Use `Urban Oasis`, `Palm Shade`, or `Morning Trust` from `craft/daylight-filters-and-instagram-grades.md`.

Avoid:

```text
tropical jungle greenery, European forest park, fantasy garden, over-saturated artificial grass, empty luxury resort landscaping, harsh noon glare, blown white sky
```

## Sports Fields And Play Areas

Observed sports and recreation cues:

- small football field inside neighborhood fabric
- blue court surfaces
- organized running/walking track around field edges
- fencing or low boundary lines
- adjacent shade structures
- surrounding sidewalks and planted zones
- aerial or high-angle documentation-style photography

Best prompt language:

```text
neighborhood sports field inside a Saudi residential community, green artificial-turf football pitch, blue multi-use court surface, walking track, shaded canopies, organized park hardscape, nearby low-rise housing, public quality-of-life infrastructure
```

Avoid:

```text
professional stadium scale, crowded arena, school gymnasium, generic Western playground, random amusement park equipment
```

## Walkways And Mobility DNA

Walkability appears as a practical community feature, not a dense urban downtown lifestyle scene.

Observed cues:

- curved pedestrian paths
- blue or colored path borders
- clean sidewalks and planted buffers
- pathways around parks and sports areas
- occasional cycling or running path feel
- broad clear circulation rather than crowded streets

Best prompt language:

```text
curving pedestrian walkway through a Saudi residential community park, clean paving, planted edges, subtle blue-green path accents, young trees, low-rise housing in the background, realistic neighborhood mobility and quality of life
```

Avoid:

```text
Manhattan sidewalk crowd, European cafe street, neon smart mobility overlay, futuristic pods, sci-fi transit
```

## City Development Layer

The broader city-development folders point to more than finished homes. They include:

- sales-center openings
- ministerial or executive visits
- project launches and groundbreakings
- signed agreements
- green initiative coverage
- community master plans
- suburban districts expanding around Saudi cities

Use these cues when the concept is institutional or LinkedIn-facing:

```text
Saudi housing and urban development milestone, officials and project teams in a contemporary residential district or exhibition setting, master-planned community context, credible public-sector achievement, organized media coverage mood, clear space for campaign text
```

For public social media visuals, prioritize place and citizen benefit. For LinkedIn or YouTube visuals, allow more institutional cues such as officials, project boards, site visits, press coverage, and partnership moments.

## Human Presence Guidance

Many source images are underpopulated or purely architectural. For campaign generation, add believable people carefully.

Recommended people:

- Saudi family walking in a neighborhood park
- parent and child near a playground or walking path
- elderly person seated in shade
- young adults using a sports field or walkway
- municipal or housing worker in the background
- engineer or project representative for development milestones

Use the diversity add-on from `craft/human-character-diversity.md`.

Best prompt language:

```text
culturally accurate Saudi people with believable variation in age, height, body type, facial features, grooming, modest clothing details, and social roles; ordinary public-service realism, no fashion-model casting, no identical faces
```

Avoid:

```text
fashion-model family, over-styled luxury lifestyle, identical faces, incorrect Saudi clothing, crowded festival scene unless the brief calls for an event
```

## Source Photography Weaknesses To Correct

The reference folders are useful for understanding reality, but many images feel documentary or amateur. Do not copy their weaknesses.

Common weaknesses to improve:

- harsh daylight and overexposed sky
- weak horizon control
- overly high or purely documentary angles
- empty spaces without human story
- unclear focal subject
- repetitive documentation shots
- weak foreground/midground/background layering
- no deliberate headline space
- hard shadows and yellow heat
- insufficient sense of civic polish

Professionalize with:

- lower, more intentional camera height for human-scale scenes
- clean architectural verticals
- early morning or softened daylight
- clear foreground civic detail
- strong midground human benefit
- background housing or district context
- disciplined negative space for Arabic headline
- restrained municipal green and teal accents
- polished but believable color grade

## Recommended Camera Language

### Hero Housing Scene

```text
35mm or 45mm lens, eye-level or slightly low civic campaign angle, clean verticals, warm Saudi morning daylight, residential facade in background, family-scale walkway or garden foreground, clear negative space for Arabic headline
```

### Park And Quality Of Life Scene

```text
24mm to 35mm lens, human-height perspective, curving path leading line, shaded seating or young trees in midground, low-rise housing in distance, Urban Oasis daylight grade
```

### Masterplan Or City Development Scene

```text
elevated but composed view, organized district geometry, readable roads and green corridors, low-rise residential clusters, controlled sky, premium public-sector campaign finish
```

### Sports And Walkability Scene

```text
wide but not distorted lens, field or walkway as leading geometry, few believable residents using the space, shaded canopies and housing context, clear civic optimism
```

## Campaign Composition Models

### From Home To City

```text
foreground: residential walkway or park detail
midground: Saudi family or citizen benefit moment
background: low-rise housing community and wider city development
headline space: clean sky, wall, or soft neutral zone
```

### Platform Awareness Series

Use each platform as a different lens on the same ecosystem:

- Instagram: visual transformations, parks, housing facades, neighborhood details
- YouTube: walkthroughs, project stories, milestones, behind-the-scenes development
- LinkedIn: partnerships, investment, agreements, masterplans, institutional progress
- Snapchat: field visits, daily city moments, quick community scenes
- Facebook: community updates and public information
- WhatsApp or service channels: practical alerts, service links, citizen access

### Achievement Plus Follow

```text
show one concrete achievement, then explain why following another platform gives a deeper view of that achievement
```

## Quality Checklist

Before accepting a generated visual:

- Does it look like a Saudi planned residential community, not a generic suburb?
- Are the homes low-rise with warm neutral facades and practical Saudi privacy logic?
- Is the public realm visible: sidewalk, park, path, sports area, or planted edge?
- Does it connect house, neighborhood, and city benefit?
- Is the daylight comfortable rather than harsh?
- Is there clean headline space?
- Are people believable and culturally accurate if present?
- Does the image feel public-sector credible rather than luxury real-estate advertising?
- Is the composition more professional than the source documentation photos?
- Are there no fake logos, fake Arabic, or inaccurate architectural motifs?
