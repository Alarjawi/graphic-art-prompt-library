# Ministry of Municipalities and Housing Visual DNA

## Identity

- Arabic name: وزارة البلديات والإسكان
- English name: Ministry of Municipalities and Housing
- Website: https://momah.gov.sa/en
- Sector: Saudi public sector, municipal services, housing, urban quality, digital government services
- Core promise: integrated municipal services and housing products for Saudi cities

## Brand Role

The ministry sits between daily city life and national transformation. Its visual language should feel public-service oriented, credible, urban, practical, and future-facing. It should communicate better cities, easier digital services, housing enablement, municipal reliability, and quality-of-life improvement.

## Strategic Tone

- civic confidence
- service clarity
- urban development
- digital accessibility
- housing stability
- institutional trust
- Vision 2030 transformation
- practical optimism

Avoid luxury-fashion drama, exaggerated futurism, generic smart-city cliches, or overly decorative national motifs.

## Visual World

Use a contemporary Saudi civic world: Riyadh streets, municipalities, public spaces, housing communities, service centers, urban planning contexts, construction and inspection environments, family housing journeys, clean city operations, and digital service touchpoints.

The world should be real, organized, and humane. It should show systems that work, not abstract technology floating in empty space.

## Color DNA

Primary cues from the provided logo and website presence:

- municipal green: growth, public service, environment, city quality
- teal: digital services, modern access, operational clarity
- white: government transparency, clean interface space
- charcoal/black: readable institutional typography
- warm stone neutrals: Saudi urban architecture and housing environments

Recommended prompt palette:

```text
municipal green and teal accents, clean white interface space, charcoal typography zones, warm Saudi stone neutrals, restrained civic color system
```

Avoid:

```text
cheap neon green, over-saturated turquoise, random rainbow gradients, dark dystopian palettes, excessive gold
```

## Typography And Layout Logic

- clear bilingual hierarchy
- clean government website structure
- large readable headline zones
- service-card logic
- accessible spacing
- disciplined alignment
- icon-led service navigation
- restrained institutional typography

For generated campaign visuals, leave clean negative space for Arabic or English headlines instead of asking the model to invent text.

## Image Style

Preferred:

- cinematic but realistic Saudi public-sector photography
- contemporary Saudi citizens and families
- municipal workers, inspectors, engineers, and service staff when relevant
- real city infrastructure, roads, sidewalks, parks, housing developments, and public facilities
- bright daylight or controlled warm civic lighting
- clean foreground, useful midground activity, recognizable Saudi urban context
- polished but believable government campaign finish

Avoid:

- generic stock-photo business handshakes
- fake Arabic text
- random logos or badges
- inaccurate Saudi clothing or architecture
- empty futuristic skylines without municipal relevance
- plastic skin, overprocessed HDR, or surreal smart-city overlays

## Composition DNA

Use compositions that show the relationship between people, services, and place.

Recommended structures:

```text
single hero citizen or service worker, Saudi urban background, clear headline space, foreground municipal detail, midground service action, background city or housing context
```

```text
family housing moment, clean residential environment, warm daylight, stable composition, negative space for campaign message, subtle municipal green and teal accents
```

```text
digital service journey, citizen using phone or kiosk, real municipal context, interface implied but not text-heavy, organized service-card visual rhythm
```

## Campaign Themes

Good ministry campaign territories:

- digital municipal services
- housing ownership and family stability
- city cleanliness and visual distortion treatment
- public service complaints and suggestions
- construction permits and building compliance
- investment opportunities and Furas platform
- Balady and Sakani service journeys
- smart guide and immediate electronic services
- quality of life in Saudi cities
- Vision 2030 urban transformation

```text
premium Saudi government campaign key visual for [service/topic], Ministry of Municipalities and Housing visual DNA, practical civic confidence, contemporary Saudi city or housing context, culturally accurate people and architecture, municipal green and teal accents, clean white and warm stone neutrals, clear hero subject, service-oriented composition, disciplined headline space, cinematic realistic daylight, polished public-sector finish, no fake Arabic text, no random logos, no generic stock-photo look, no inaccurate Saudi clothing or architecture, no overprocessed HDR
```
